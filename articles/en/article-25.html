OpenAI Jalapeño Chip Launch: The War on NVIDIA Dominance Begins

OpenAI unveils first self-developed AI chip, 9-month partnership with Broadcom, inference-only design — "using AI to design chips that run AI"

On June 25, 2026, OpenAI officially unveiled its first self-developed AI chip Jalapeño in partnership with Broadcom. Following Google TPU, Amazon Trainium, and Microsoft Maia, another tech giant enters the AI chip race. Everyone suffers from NVIDIA dominance — an inference chip war is fully underway.

I. 9 Months of Speed: From Concept to Silicon

OpenAI revealed the entire process from chip conception to physical delivery took only 9 months — remarkably fast in the semiconductor industry. Traditional chips from design to mass production often take 3-5 years or longer.

Jalapeño's core design philosophy is "inference-only" — unlike existing AI chips optimized for training, Jalapeño is exclusively designed for "exam-taking" without the "test preparation" work. Its single goal: answer user questions fastest with least power consumption.

"Jalapeño's per-watt performance significantly exceeds current market best results."

— OpenAI Official Disclosure

OpenAI stated early testing shows Jalapeño's per-watt performance significantly exceeds current market best results, with detailed data to be published in a technical report within months. Jalapeño is expected to begin large-scale deployment by end of 2026.

II. Using AI to Design Chips that Run AI

Notably, the chip design process utilized OpenAI's own AI to assist acceleration. This follows Anthropic's similar strategy of using AI to design chip architectures.

"Using AI to design chips that run AI" truly represents "fighting magic with magic." This marks AI penetrating from application to infrastructure layer, as AI companies use their own technological capabilities to redefine chip design processes.

III. Everyone Suffers from NVIDIA Dominance

Analysts believe OpenAI's core motivation is cost reduction. Currently Anthropic pays $1.25 billion monthly to rent compute from Musk, while H200 prices in China have risen to over 4 million yuan.

"Everyone suffers from NVIDIA dominance" — each vendor seeks alternatives. OpenAI CEO Sam Altman has publicly stated multiple times that AI chip acquisition costs are a key bottleneck constraining AI development.

IV. Inference Chip War Begins

Jalapeño's launch signals the full commencement of the inference chip war. Unlike training chips pursuing extreme compute, inference chips pursue efficiency — fastest response speed within limited power and cost.

This trend highly aligns with current AI application evolution. In 2026, AI is shifting paradigm from "chatbot" to "agent" — AI no longer just answers questions but executes tasks in real-time. This raises inference speed requirements.

When AI begins working for humans, inference efficiency directly determines user experience and business value. Jalapeño targets precisely this critical inflection point.

V. Outlook: Is the Pepper Hot Enough?

Jalapeño is expected to begin large-scale deployment by year-end, with actual results yet to be verified. Chip-making is an extremely capital-intensive and prolonged business, with many companies paying tuition on this path historically.

But if OpenAI wins the bet, the first to feel the change will be every user of these tools daily. Future ChatGPT may become cheaper and faster.

Is this "pepper" hot enough? Stay tuned.
