New Landscape of AI LLM Competition in 2026: Anthropic Valuation Peaks, Embodied AI Era Begins

Anthropic completes $65B funding reaching $965B valuation, surpassing OpenAI for the first time. MiniMax M3 coding surpasses GPT-5.5. AI LLM competition enters a new phase.

📅 Published June 3, 2026 📖 8 min read 🏷️ LLM
Key Takeaway: When Anthropic surpassed OpenAI with a $65 billion funding round and $965 billion valuation, it wasn't just a number change — it was a landmark event marking AI competition entering a new phase. The AI industry in 2026 is undergoing profound structural reshaping.

💰 1. Anthropic Valuation Peaks: 4000x Revenue Growth in Four Years

On May 28, Anthropic announced completion of a $65 billion Series H funding round with a post-money valuation of $965 billion. This figure not only surpassed OpenAI but also made Anthropic officially the world's highest-valued artificial intelligence company.

Anthropic CEO Dario Amodei disclosed that the company's annual revenue has grown from $10 million in 2022 to over $440 billion in May 2026 — a 4000-fold increase in four years. This stunning growth rate is primarily driven by the widespread adoption of the Claude series models in the enterprise market.

💡 Investor Lineup

This round attracted Micron, Samsung, and SK Hynix as co-investors — storage chip giants with significant influence in AI computing infrastructure.

🤖 2. Claude Opus 4.8: The Winning Formula for Enterprise Market

Shortly after the funding announcement, Anthropic officially released Claude Opus 4.8 on May 29 — another iterative update just one month after Opus 4.7.

"Claude Opus 4.8 achieved 69.2% on SWE-Bench Pro, surpassing other mainstream models of the same type on multiple benchmarks."
— Anthropic Official Announcement

Core Upgrade: Agent Collaboration Capabilities

The core upgrade of Claude Opus 4.8 lies in agent collaboration capabilities:

  • Dynamic Workflows: Supports coordinating hundreds of parallel sub-agents to complete large-scale complex tasks
  • Improved Honesty: Probability of undetected code bugs is only one-quarter of Opus 4.7
  • Fast Mode: Running speed increased to 2.5x original, costs reduced to one-third

More notably, Anthropic announced that Claude Mythos Preview (with alignment and reasoning capabilities considered the current architecture ceiling) will be opened to all customers "within weeks." This indicates Anthropic is accelerating the market release of its most advanced technology.

🇨🇳 3. Rise of Chinese LLMs: MiniMax M3 Breakthrough

Across the Pacific, Chinese AI company MiniMax released its new generation general model MiniMax M3 on June 1, attracting industry attention.

💡 Technical Breakthrough

MiniMax M3 is China's first large model simultaneously equipped with three core capabilities: "frontier coding ability, 1M ultra-long context, and native multimodal."

Sparse Attention Architecture: 20x Efficiency Improvement

MiniMax M3 uses its self-developed sparse attention architecture MiniMax Sparse Attention (MSA). At 1 million context scale, per-token computation is only about 1/20 of the previous generation model, with significantly improved inference efficiency.

  • Surpassed OpenAI GPT-5.5 and Google Gemini 3.1 Pro on SWE-Bench Pro programming capability benchmark
  • Approaching Claude Opus 4.7 level
  • 15x inference speed improvement at 1M context

Accelerating Capital Moves

On the same day, MiniMax announced the start of A-share IPO tutoring, targeting the STAR Market listing. This is another major capital decision just six months after its Hong Kong listing. Data shows MiniMax has approximately 300 million global users, with annual recurring revenue doubling over the past two months to over $300 million.

🦾 4. Embodied AI Year Begins: OpenAI Officially Enters

If LLM competition is the software-level contest, then embodied AI represents the strategic high ground of AI extending into the physical world. In June 2026, this field welcomed a heavyweight player — OpenAI officially announced the establishment of its Robotics division.

OpenAI's Robotics Strategy

Sam Altman revealed OpenAI's robotics strategy in two steps:

  1. Short-term goal: "Assistance robots" — helping technical workers build infrastructure
  2. Long-term vision: Giving everyone a personal robot

Notably, OpenAI has transferred its world simulation research project entirely to the robotics direction, led by DALL-E inventor and Sora core developer Aditya Ramesh. This indicates OpenAI is transforming from a pure software company to "software-hardware integration."

💡 Business Considerations

OpenAI's current valuation is approximately $1 trillion, with IPO expected in September. The robotics narrative provides new imagination space for listing valuation.
