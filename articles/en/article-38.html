2. DeepSeek Economics: Half of the RMB 51B Round May Flow to ASIC

Why does DeepSeek build silicon now? The answer isn't «patriotism» or «strategic defense»—it's a hard economic ledger. DeepSeek R2 / V4 inference volume hit approximately 65% of OpenAI's total in Q2 2026—translating OpenRouter and Vercel disclosures, DeepSeek's monthly inference lease exceeds USD 250M, annualizing beyond USD 3B. No non-hyperscaler on earth can absorb that inference-cost pressure indefinitely.

The economics run on three lines: (1) Cut inference cost another 30-50%—industry estimates suggest an ASIC deeply optimized for DeepSeek's own model (MoE + long context) can theoretically deliver 30-50% lower TCO than H800. (2) Sidestep EO 14232 H800 / H20 export controls—after EO 14232 took effect on June 26 and US chip export restrictions tightened again, H20 supply visibility deteriorated; without its own inference silicon, DeepSeek faces a potential «inference capacity cutoff» risk in 2027. (3) Post-close cash after the RMB 51B round—DeepSeek closed a RMB 51B round in June 2026 (valuation USD 52-59B), giving it enough cash to cover the USD 300-500M first tape-out threshold; industry estimates put subsequent mass-production spend at USD 1.5-2B, well within its capacity.

3. Zhipu's 27x GLM-5.2 Surge: ASIC as the Only Way Out

Zhipu walks the same path as DeepSeek, but with a completely different trigger. DeepSeek's driver is «existing scale making GPU rental unbearable»; Zhipu's driver is «overnight growth outpacing GPU rental». Public Vercel data shows GLM-5.2's first-week daily-token consumption surged 27x, with customers up 80x; OpenRouter data ranks GLM-5.2 in the US API top 5, and still rising.

What does 27x mean? Continuing to lease Nvidia / Ascend inference capacity would take Zhipu's monthly inference bill from ~RMB 300M to over RMB 2B—beyond its operating cash-flow tolerance. Going ASIC instead of GPGPU lets Zhipu co-design silicon around GLM's architecture (MoE + long context). Industry estimates put Zhipu's ASIC first tape-out at H1 2027 and mass production at H2 2028. If executed on schedule, Zhipu becomes the world's third company—after Google TPU and Amazon Trainium—to close the «model + chip» full-stack loop.

Zhipu's move isn't perfectly aligned with DeepSeek's. DeepSeek runs a «cost-efficient inference service» playbook; Zhipu runs a «model + platform + Agent full-stack» playbook—two paths point to two chip architectures. DeepSeek's ASIC leans toward «high-throughput, low-latency inference»; Zhipu's ASIC likely embeds «agent-loop invocation optimization» (KV cache reuse, multi-turn context compression). Signals already in the market: technical engagement between Zhipu and Moore Threads, Iluvatar CoreX has been visible since April; The Information's July 7 report confirms the ASIC path.

5. Nvidia's USD 1T May Peak Evaporation: Three Drivers of the Valuation Collapse

«De-Nvidiafication» isn't abstract narrative—it's already priced into the stock. Nvidia has retraced 16% from its May 14, 2026 peak, roughly USD 1 trillion of market cap gone, forward PE at 18x—the lowest since 2019. S&P 500 forward PE sits at 20x, Nasdaq 100 near 23x. YTD Nvidia is up only 5.6%, materially trailing S&P 500 (+9.6%) and Nasdaq 100 (+16%), ranking third-from-bottom among 30 major semis.

Three drivers behind the valuation collapse: (1) «Customer CapEx tightening» fears—Microsoft, Meta, and Google didn't uniformly raise 2027 CapEx guidance on their late-June calls, and Meta's Compute project (announced early June) has the tape worrying that MTIA silicon will divert GPU orders. (2) The «non-Nvidia camp» is materially landing—SambaNova / Groq / Cerebras / Rebellions all closed rounds or wins in the same week, giving the market its first credible «alternatives narrative». (3) The AI-app ROI mood, especially after OpenAI Sol's half-price launch and Chinese open models grabbing 46% of the US API market, is producing a strange pattern where downstream customers use more AI but see flat bills.

Fundamentals, however, have not cracked—Nvidia still shows 37 of 37 analysts on Buy or Hold, single-quarter GM 74.2%, OP margin 64%, FY2027 revenue guidance USD 393B, Goldman targets USD 285, Bank of America USD 350. This looks more like macro re-pricing than company re-pricing—the market isn't newly bearish on fundamentals; it is no longer willing to pay a premium for «exclusive AI compute for the next five years». That scarcity premium underwrote a 30x rally since CUDA went abroad in 2020, and it is now being systematically corrected.

6. Software-side De-Nvidiafication: Chinese Open Models at 46% US API

Hardware-side de-Nvidiafication needs another two years before mass production; software-side de-Nvidiafication, in the same week, has already achieved substantive penetration. CNBC and OpenRouter jointly released data on July 7, 2026: since February 8, Chinese open models have held a 30%+ weekly share of the US API market on OpenRouter, peaking at 46%, with the trailing-12-month average rising from H1 2025's 4.5% to 2026's 11%. GLM 5.2 scores 62.1 on SWE-bench Pro (GPT-5.5: 58.6); Vercel data shows GLM 5.2 saw daily tokens +27x and customers +80x in week one. Lindy switched 100% to DeepSeek in June, estimating «several million saved per month»; Coinbase now defaults to GLM / Kimi in its support stack; Airbnb's customer service stack switched to Qwen 3.

Chinese models are 60-90% cheaper than closed peers and ship under MIT / Apache 2.0 licenses. Yacine Jernite, Hugging Face's ML Head, backs the open-model path in his CNBC interview: MIT license + open weights + self-hosting means enterprises can deploy on-prem, meet GDPR offline, and land compute cost at ~1/10 of API pricing. The path of software-side de-Nvidiafication connects like this: open models permit self-hosting → self-hosting can run on any hardware (not necessarily H100) → therefore Groq, SambaNova, Cerebras, AWS Trainium all become viable substrates → Nvidia's software-hardware dual moat is torn open from the software side.

The structural implication for US labs: on the day GPT-5.6 opens globally, its real competitor is not Anthropic or Google—it is Chinese open source. Justin Summerville, OpenRouter's Data & Analytics Lead, told CNBC bluntly: «60-90% cheaper is the first driver of this penetration wave.» This «price-before-brand» path mirrors Linux breaking through commercial Unix in the 2004-2010 era. Industry consensus: if GLM 5.2 and DeepSeek V4 sustain this pace in H2 2026, Chinese open models could take 55-60% of the US API market by end-2027.

7. Can the CUDA Moat Hold? Dual Loop vs. 20 Years of Ecosystem

The question ultimately returns to a single core: will Nvidia's 20-year CUDA software-hardware dual moat be breached by this domino? First, unpack the moat: (1) CUDA programming model + cuDNN + cuBLAS + NCCL software stack—4 million CUDA developers globally, 200,000 CUDA projects, and default backend across nearly every mainstream AI framework (PyTorch / TensorFlow / JAX); (2) NVLink + InfiniBand multi-card interconnect—the Nvidia-GPU-plus-Mellanox-NIC cluster performance advantage is not easily reproduced in the short term; (3) DGX / HGX system-level design—the full reference architecture from cooling to power to rack design lets new customers stand up AI clusters at marginal cost far below DIY; (4) Deep TSMC 4nm binding—Blackwell and next-gen Rubin ride TSMC CoWoS-L capacity, and Nvidia is the largest buyer; other challengers cannot access equivalent capacity in the short term.

Three impact paths deserve honest assessment: impact 1—«Triton + torch.compile flatten kernel differences»—OpenAI's Triton compiler lets developers write kernel code once and compile to CUDA / ROCm / Trainium / MTIA backends. PyTorch 2.6 is deeply integrating this, theoretically reducing CUDA's software exclusivity by 30-50%. Impact 2—«cloud-provider full-stack packaging»—AWS Neuron, Google Cloud TPU, and Microsoft Maia are all «chip + framework + service» managed offerings, sparing customers from touching CUDA. Impact 3—«software complexity drops in the Agent era»—Agent workloads are primarily model-inference plus tool-calling, with far less need for CUDA kernel customization than training. Together, these paths systematically weaken the CUDA moat on the inference side while keeping a 3-5 year lead on the training side.

The conclusion is relative: the CUDA moat won't be breached, but shifts from «single-choke-point» to «multi-front defense». Nvidia will retain 80%+ share in training, but inference share may drop from 90% to 60-70%—already enough to slow Nvidia's revenue growth from 2026's +70% to 2027's +30-35%, and enough to move the valuation percentile from 2024's 95th all-time percentile back to 2026's 60th. That is the industry-level explanation of Nvidia's USD 1T evaporation.

8. Investment Map's Third Rewriting: From Nvidia to Other Semis to Cloud and Inference ASIC

The de-Nvidiafication domino kicks off the third rewriting of the AI investment map. First rewriting (2023-2024) moved from «buy Nvidia» to «buy Nvidia + TSMC + memory + optical modules»—full-supply-chain re-rating for AI silicon, benefiting SK Hynix, Micron, Vertiv, Coherent, and Ciena. Second rewriting (2025-H1 2026) moved from «buy hardware» to «buy hardware + cloud»—Morgan Stanley's July 6 «AI: Ten Investment Truths» report explicitly stated «sell chips, buy cloud», with Oracle Cloud, Palantir, and ServiceNow outperforming Nvidia.

This week of July 2026 kicks off the third rewriting: from «buy hardware + cloud» to «buy inference ASIC + Agent apps + Chinese open-source ecosystem». SambaNova at USD 11B, JPMorgan orders, DeepSeek + Zhipu chips, and Chinese models at 46% US API—these four converged in one week, and investors are for the first time systematically thinking about «non-Nvidia» exposure. Industry consensus points to three main vectors: (1) Direct challenger-chip stocks—SambaNova (2027 IPO planned), Cerebras (Q4 2026 IPO), Rebellions (Korea-listed), Groq (USD 1.5B closed, USD 4B valuation); (2) Challenger-serving cloud middlemen—Together AI, Fireworks, Anyscale offering Chinese open-model API layers; (3) Chip-design tools and foundry chain—Cadence, Synopsys, GlobalFoundries, SMIC absorbing custom-ASIC demand.

To be clear, «buy Nvidia» isn't dead—but from H2 2026, it is no longer the only «must-win» AI exposure. 18x forward PE is a seven-year low, but fundamentals haven't cracked—this is a «valuation-percentile repricing» stage, not a «fundamental repricing» stage. A rational investor's strategy: keep Nvidia as core, at reduced weight (from 25-30% to 15-18%), add challenger chip stocks and inference-ASIC cloud services, retain AWS / Azure / Google Cloud / Oracle Cloud as the «AI cloud» core, and lean into Agent-native software (Palantir / ServiceNow / CrowdStrike). That «multi-point diversified» allocation is the most rational asset-allocation response to the de-Nvidiafication domino.

Conclusion: The Domino Has Begun to Fall—Don't Mistake It for Nvidia's Death Knell

July 7-8, 2026 marked the global AI compute ecosystem's deepest structural repricing since CUDA went abroad in 2020. DeepSeek + Zhipu chip programs put Chinese foundation-model companies on the third path to «model + chip» closed-loop (after Google TPU and Amazon Trainium); SambaNova at USD 11B and JPMorgan orders give the «non-Nvidia camp» its first mainstream financial-capital endorsement; Chinese open models at 46% US API penetration bring software-side de-Nvidiafication two years earlier than expected; Nvidia's USD 1T market-cap evaporation reflects the market pricing in a 40% growth slowdown over the next three years.

But de-Nvidiafication does not equal Nvidia collapse. On the training side Nvidia will keep 80%+ share into 2028; Rubin and successor architectures will maintain their edge at the generative-AI inference cluster frontier. What is actually being repriced is «Nvidia's valuation percentile»—from 2024's 95th all-time percentile to 2026's 60-65th. A higher valuation discount rate turns Nvidia's market-cap curve from «steep upward» to «choppy upward». Three iron laws to remember: (1) The domino won't fall overnight—DeepSeek and Zhipu ASIC mass production waits until 2028; (2) Valuation re-rating is not fundamental collapse—Nvidia's single-quarter GM is still 74.2%, all 37 analysts on Buy or Hold; (3) The AI investment map is moving from «single dominant player» to «multi-path diversification»—rational portfolios should simultaneously bet on Nvidia + challengers + cloud + Agent apps. That is the core investment lesson written by «de-Nvidiafication domino day»—July 9, 2026.
