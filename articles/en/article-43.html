II. Policy Foundations: From 'Growth Stabilization' to 'Structural Reconstruction'

To understand why policy is binding 'AI' and 'consumption' together, look first at the numbers underneath—

China's 2025 retail sales totaled RMB 48.79 trillion. The State Council's 15th Five-Year Consumption Plan sets the 2030 target at RMB 60 trillion—implying a 4.2% CAGR over the next 5 years. That is no small ask against weakening traditional consumption drivers (appliances, autos, catering, apparel) and continued real-estate adjustment—China must find new, policy-elastic, mass-scalable, and export-ready consumption engines.

The Plan's top-priority direction is 'deepen AI+Consumption'—explicitly requiring 'greater effective supply of AI phones and PCs, smart wearables, intelligent robots, and desktop 3D printers'. The 8-ministry Implementation Opinions spell out the toolbox in blunt detail—

  • Demand side: digital product trade-in vouchers, green trade-in, and, for the first time, individual consumer-loan interest subsidies covering AI product purchases—the heavyweight punch of the toolbox.
  • Supply side: intelligent terminal supply, AI content and services, AI+Education, AI+Healthcare, AI+Culture/Tourism, AI+Home, AI+Transportation, AI+Elderly Care.
  • Institutional: data marketization, algorithm governance, consumer rights, cross-ministry regulatory coordination.
  • Infrastructure: intelligent-compute networks, open large-model platforms, public-data authorized operations.
  • Internationalization: streamlined export of AI consumer goods and AI service-trade promotion.

The Three-Lever Stack of the AI+Consumption Toolbox

1. Digital product trade-in vouchers—direct rebate on qualifying purchases; in 2026 the scope expands to AI phones, AI PCs, smart wearables, and desktop robots.
2. Green trade-in—old smart devices credited toward new-device purchases in a circular loop.
3. Individual consumer-loan interest subsidy—for the first time covering AI product purchases, effectively 'borrow to buy an AI device, and the state pays your interest'. This is the first time policy elevates AI consumption to 'strategic consumer goods' status.

Market signals have already appeared—MOFCOM big data show that in Jan-Apr 2026 online sales of smart mobility exoskeletons rose +785.5% YoY, smart glasses +175.2% (April), humanoid robots +20.6%—all three tracks showing triple-digit-plus explosive growth. Shanghai University of Finance & Economics' Consumption Big-Data Center forecasts trillion-yuan-scale annual incremental consumption from AI+Consumption—if the forecast holds, AI consumer goods will be the single largest incremental source over the 15th Five-Year period.

III. Terminal-Hardware Duet: Chip and Device Debut on the Same Day

Beneath the policy blueprint published on July 13 sits the same-day launch of two Shanghai hardware events—Zhangjiang and Xuhui—that structurally support the AI+Consumption thesis.

Act I: Dongfang Suanxin DF1000—World's First 3D-Stacked Near-Memory AI Chip on Domestic 14nm

Shanghai Zhangjiang's Dongfang Suanxin launched and began volume production of DF1000 on July 13—the world's first 3D-stacked near-memory AI chip built on domestic mature 14nm with software-defined architecture. The core breakthrough combines '3D hybrid-bonding stack + near-memory compute'—bonding memory and compute units onto the same silicon stack, cutting data-movement power by 65%+ (data-movement is ~60% of H100 power), reducing compute latency by 40%, and lifting area-normalized compute density by nearly 3x. 'Software-defined' means the chip can reconfigure compute-storage hierarchy per workload, spanning LLM inference, edge compute, and robotics.

This is a systemic breakthrough in China's 'process-detour + architecture-innovation' response to the EUV lithography ban—rather than chasing the leading node, use 3D packaging and near-memory architecture to achieve a system-level generational leap. Dongfang Suanxin's CEO Wei Shaojun is the former chairman of CSIA's IC Design Branch; the company was seed-funded by Zhangjiang Hi-Tech in July 2024 and received a Series A led by the National AI Industry Investment Fund in 2025. The roadmap is crisp—DF2000 (4,000 TFLOPS@FP4) in Q4 2026 and DF3000 (8,000 TFLOPS@FP4, 20 TB/s memory bandwidth) in Q4 2027—DF3000's specs approach Nvidia's B300 per-card level. Shanghai Pudong's IC industry topped RMB 360 billion in 2025 (+23%). This launch moves 'Shanghai-made AI chips' from follower to architecture-innovation leader.

Act II: StepFun STEPX Neo + Step AOS + Step Amoo—LLM-Native AI Terminals

The same day, in Shanghai Xuhui, StepFun launched STEPX—the world's first LLM-native AI terminal brand—along with the flagship agentic phone STEPX Neo, the agent-native OS Step AOS, and the personal agent Step Amoo. Step AOS has three core innovations: (1) a dual-domain three-tier memory architecture separating 'immediate / working / long-term' memory across user and task domains, delivering daily-Q&A recall at up to 15 ms; (2) an edge-cloud multi-brain architecture running lightweight agents on-device while connecting to the Step Pro flagship reasoning model (>1.5T parameters) in the cloud; (3) a four-dimensional safety framework—trustworthy, visible, controllable, reversible—where every agent action is traceable and reversible.

On the hard-metric side, STEPX Neo has passed the first-batch L3-grade certification of the CAICT-led 'AI-Terminal Intelligence Rating'—the current industry ceiling. On the ecosystem side, StepFun has completed deep API integration with 10 top apps: Ctrip, Alipay, Didi, Meituan, WPS, CapCut, AutoNavi, JD, Baidu, Weibo—letting users complete flight bookings, food orders, and cross-app content organization from a single sentence. At the launch, Chairman Yin Qi announced a 100-Day Co-Creation Program opening Step AOS to global developers and hardware partners, targeting an Android-style ecosystem explosion.

"Step AOS is our first complete answer to what an 'agent-first' operating system should look like—not a layer of AI paint over a traditional OS, but agents as native first-class citizens."

— Yin Qi, Chairman of StepFun, Shanghai Launch Event, July 13, 2026

The industry signal from both launches is the same: from 'cloud APIs' to 'consumer-grade terminals'—for the first time, Chinese LLM companies have jumped out of the model arms race and put agents inside AI phones. The policy language of 'AI phones/AI PCs/smart wearables' finally has concrete, deployable, mass-producible, and globalizable product referents.

VI. Investment & Risk Checklist: Opportunities and Traps Coexist

The 'AI Consumer Year' window opens, but risk never disappears. Three investment themes and three risk lists—

Three Investment Themes

Theme 1: 'Co-invest' terminal OEM + LLM company. The StepFun playbook (model + terminal + OS + ecosystem) is validated; expect more combos like 'Doubao + ByteDance AI phone' or 'Kimi + hardware partner'. On the terminal side, favor brand-strong, channel-mature top OEMs; on the model side, favor top-tier players with edge-cloud integrated capability.

Theme 2: Domestic AI chips + 3D packaging / near-memory compute. Dongfang Suanxin's DF1000 proves that 'not chasing the leading node' can still deliver system-level miracles through 'process-detour + architecture-innovation'. Upstream supply chains in HBM, chiplets, 3D packaging, and optical interconnects will benefit persistently.

Theme 3: Humanoid robotics + AI healthcare, the two most policy-elastic tracks. Humanoid robotics is driven by subsidies + B2B substitution + B2C companionship; AI healthcare rides the new narrative of 'AI pharma + consumer medicine' opened by Insilico's Phase III. Both offer '10x space + 5-year runway' characteristics.

Three Risk Lists

  • Safety and governance pressure: FLI's H1 2026 AI Safety Index (released July 7) shows Anthropic at C+ (highest), OpenAI/Google DeepMind both C, xAI/DeepSeek/Mistral all F; no company scored A or B; four labs weakened or abandoned prior 'red-line' commitments. Chinese regulators could tighten red lines on specific AI consumer scenarios (minor companionship, AI healthcare, AI-glasses privacy) at any time.
  • Rapid product homogenization: AI phones and AI PCs' 'model spec + camera + screen' spec sheets are easy to copy in 3-6 months, opening major price-war risk. STEPX Neo is a first-mover, but if ecosystem lock-in is not fast enough, the head start can be neutralized by later entrants' price wars.
  • Subsidy taper and policy cycle: interest subsidies, trade-in vouchers, and green trade-in are strong policy stimuli—when they taper, demand may face visible pull-forward payback. The 'policy honeymoon' typically runs 12-24 months; secondary markets must position ahead for 'post-honeymoon' commercialization stress tests.

The Deep Difference Between the Two Paradigms

The US paradigm bets on 'stronger models, more chips, further upstream'—characterized by 'AGI-first, compute-intensive, governance-anxious'.
The Chinese paradigm bets on 'get AI into every Chinese household, every phone, every robot, every medical visit'—characterized by 'consumer-first, policy-intensive, scenario-rich'.
Neither paradigm is absolutely right or wrong—but they define the divergence point for the next 5 years of the two AI superpowers. China's choice to run the 'downstream payoff' first is a clear 'scale-then-upstream' strategy.

This is the first time within the US paradigm that an 'upstream compute vendor personally jumps down to grab downstream API market share'—arguably the first 'learning' from the US paradigm toward the Chinese one (downstream-execution oriented).
