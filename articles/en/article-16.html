2. Why is Capital So Mad? Three Underlying Logics

To understand this capital frenzy, we need to deeply analyze its three underlying logics.

1. Betting on the Approach of Technological Singularity

The primary reason investment institutions are betting heavily on AI is the belief that AGI (Artificial General Intelligence) is becoming reachable. Prometheus founder Jeff Bezos stated in an interview: "We are witnessing the most important technological transformation in human history. No sober investor would sit on the sidelines during this transformation."

This belief in "technological singularity approaching" is becoming the dominant narrative in AI investment. When investors believe AGI will become reality in 5-10 years, any current valuation seems reasonable — because the winner takes all.

2. Data Sovereignty and Geopolitics

The Anthropic Fable 5 being restricted for export by the US government citing national security concerns showed the world the strategic value of AI models. When a model can be shut down due to geopolitical reasons, when AI capability becomes core to national competitiveness, countries and enterprises must begin building "AI sovereignty."

This explains why India invests in Sarvam AI, Europe supports Mistral, China develops DeepSeek — no one wants to be at a disadvantage in this critical field. Geopolitics is becoming a super accelerator for AI investment.

3. Industrial Capital's New Narrative

Tether leading NEURA Robotics investment represents another trend: industrial capital is using AI to rewrite their growth stories. Tether has hit a ceiling in the stablecoin field and needs new growth engines; Schaeffler and Bosch investing in robotics is to maintain their industry position in the Industry 4.0 era.

These industrial capitals don't care about short-term returns, focusing more on strategic positioning. This makes AI valuation logic shift from "P/E ratio" to "strategic value" — a completely different valuation system.

3. Bubble Signals: Four Indicators Worth Vigilance

However, while we celebrate this capital feast, it's necessary to stay alert. The following four indicators are worth vigilance.

⚠️ Warning Signals

The humanoid robot track raised approximately $18 billion in 2026, but the industry's total revenue is only about $340 million — the valuation-to-revenue ratio exceeds 50:1. This is a valuation bubble of over 50 times.

1. Extreme Deviation of Valuation from Revenue

Figure AI valued at $39 billion, but robot deliveries still number in the hundreds. NEURA Robotics valued at $7 billion, with mass production targets set for end of 2026, but still no named customers. This "prepaying the future" model has appeared many times in history, each accompanied by bubble bursts.

2. Shift in Investor Risk Appetite

When Tether, a cryptocurrency company, begins leading AI investments, market risk appetite has qualitatively changed. Cryptocurrency market logic is "narrative-driven" — as long as there's a story, valuations can inflate infinitely. If this logic spreads to AI investment, the consequences are unimaginable.

3. Uncertainty of Commercialization Paths

Prometheus claims developing "general AI engineers," but specific product forms remain unclear. How long until NEURA Robotics' robots are deployed in factories? How will Mistral achieve commercialization under pressure from OpenAI and Anthropic? These questions have no clear answers.

4. Accumulation of Regulatory Risks

AI regulation is becoming global consensus. EU AI Act fully implemented in August, 42 US states investigating OpenAI, UK banning minors from social media... Tightening regulation will directly impact AI companies' business models and valuation foundations.

4. Historical Mirror: Lessons from Three Tech Bubbles

Let's review three famous tech bubbles in history, which may provide some insights.

"'This time is different' is the most dangerous sentence in financial history. Before every bubble bursts, investors find reasons to convince themselves this time is different.
— Sir John Templeton

2000 Internet Bubble: Amazon fell 95% from its peak but eventually survived to become the world's largest e-commerce company. Insight: Core technology and business model are more important than valuation.

2008 Financial Crisis: Lehman Brothers went bankrupt, but JPMorgan survived. Insight: Risk management is always paramount.

2021 Cryptocurrency Bubble: FTX collapsed, but Bitcoin and Ethereum still exist. Insight: Truly valuable assets will endure cycle tests.

Returning to AI investment, the insight is equally clear: what ultimately survives cycles isn't the highest-valued company but the one that truly creates value and solves real problems.

5. Future Outlook: How to Stay Rational in the Frenzy

Facing this AI capital frenzy, how should investors and enterprises respond?

For investors: Diversify risks, don't put all eggs in one basket. Focus on company fundamentals (technological barriers, business models, management teams) rather than pure valuation numbers. Remember: after the 2000 internet bubble burst, the survivors weren't the highest-valued companies but those with the healthiest cash flow.

For AI enterprises: Stockpile sufficient ammunition while capital is abundant, but don't be dizzy with high valuations. Focus on product R&D and commercialization, building true competitive barriers. In history, many companies died from "too much financing" rather than "too little."

For policymakers: Need to find balance between encouraging innovation and preventing risks. Over-regulation stifles innovation, while letting bubbles run wild harms the entire industry and investors.

Conclusion: Embrace Change, Stay Vigilant

The AI capital frenzy of 2026 is both a driver of technological revolution and an accumulation of bubble risks. We cannot miss the dividends of the AI revolution due to fear of bubbles, nor can we ignore potential risks while chasing hotspots.

History tells us every great technological revolution is accompanied by bubbles and adjustments. After the internet bubble burst, we got Amazon, Google, and Apple; after the cryptocurrency bubble burst, we got practical applications of blockchain technology. Similarly, after the AI bubble bursts, true AI giants will emerge.

The question is: Can we stay sober in the revelry, identifying true value in the bubbles? The answer isn't predicting when bubbles burst but understanding what is truly valuable — those companies solving real problems, creating real value, building real barriers will ultimately survive cycles and become winners of the new era.
