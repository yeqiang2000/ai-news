AI Compute Revolution: From GPU Dominance to CPU Return

Industry Analysis 📅 June 23, 2026 ⏱️ 8 min read ✍️ AI News Daily

For the past few years, the global AI compute narrative has been almost exclusively dominated by NVIDIA and its GPUs. However, as AI has progressed from foundational large language models to inference applications, and now to the globally trending Agent era, chips beyond GPUs are regaining their position. This transformation has not only driven demand for Intel's Xeon server CPUs to outpace supply, but has also led Jensen Huang to actively "promote" CPUs this year—"Now, the CPU is the conductor, and the GPU is the orchestra."

1. From 1:8 to 1:1: Agents Reshape Compute Ratios

The shift in the compute market stems from AI technology's accelerated evolution from "Generative AI" to "Agentic AI." NVIDIA executives have noted that AI has officially entered the era of autonomous agents capable of "automatic execution, tool usage, and self-checking."

Traditional CPUs are designed for humans operating at the second-level, while agents "live in the nanosecond world" and are extremely sensitive to latency. Additionally, a single agent's token consumption can reach up to 1,000 times that of single-turn inference.

🔄 Fundamental Changes in Compute Ratios

Traditional LLM Training Phase: The CPU-to-GPU ratio was typically 1:8. This is because training primarily relied on GPUs' parallel computing power, with CPUs only handling data reading and task scheduling.

Agent Phase: Due to extensive task orchestration, data movement, memory coordination, and security control requirements, CPU workload has surged, with the ratio rapidly converging toward 1:1.

This growth is reflected in the financial reports of CPU giants. In Q1 this year, Intel's earnings showed data center and AI product revenue of $5.1 billion, a 22% year-over-year increase, making it the company's fastest-growing business segment. In May, AMD CEO Lisa Su announced during the earnings call that the server CPU market size forecast had doubled from $60 billion to over $120 billion.

2. New Opportunities for Chip Giants

At COMPUTEX 2026 in Taipei, Intel CEO Lip-Bu Tan revealed a telling detail: in just four weeks, multiple global tech CEO called him directly—all wanting one thing: more CPUs.

"Over the past few months, the company was still crawling, but people have started to see the potential."
— Lip-Bu Tan, Intel CEO

Intel's confidence stems from a structural reversal occurring in the global AI compute market. In the Agent era, CPUs are no longer supporting players but are taking on the critical role of "conductor"—coordinating GPUs, NPUs, and specialized accelerators to ensure agents can respond at nanosecond-level speeds.

3. The New Compute Order in the Agent Era

According to a recent report from Bernstein, a major Wall Street investment bank, agent workloads are driving structural growth in the server CPU market. By 2028, AI agent-related workloads will account for over 40% of total data center workloads.

This trend means the entire AI compute market pie is being redistributed. While GPUs remain important, the CPU's role as "conductor" is being reevaluated. As Jensen Huang stated, CPUs and GPUs working in their respective roles and collaborating effectively is what enables agents to operate efficiently.

📊 Key Data Comparison

In the traditional Generative AI phase, CPUs primarily handled data preprocessing and task distribution, with GPUs as the primary compute force. But in the Agent era, CPUs need to take on:

  • Multi-agent task orchestration and coordination
  • Real-time decision and response scheduling
  • Memory management and data flow control
  • Security verification and permission management

4. Who Will Lead the Next Phase?

The AI narrative long dominated by a single chip type is being pushed toward a new turning point. The key to this transformation lies not in the performance of any single chip, but in who can provide the best "CPU+GPU+NPU" heterogeneous computing solution.

From Intel to AMD, from NVIDIA to the many AI chip startups, a new war centered on "Agent compute" has already begun. The old order is collapsing, and the new order has not yet been established—this is the most exciting time for chip giants.

💡 Summary

The AI compute market is undergoing a structural transformation from "GPU dominance" to "CPU return." The workload characteristics of the Agent era are reshaping the CPU-to-GPU ratio from the traditional 1:8 toward 1:1. This shift not only brings new opportunities for CPU giants like Intel and AMD, but will also profoundly impact the development trajectory of the entire AI industry.

In the future, whoever can provide the optimal "conductor and orchestra" solution in the heterogeneous computing era will lead the second half of this compute revolution.
