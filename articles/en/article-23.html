# From Model Wars to Value Delivery: China's AI LLM Scale Application Leads the World China's AI large language model ecosystem is experiencing a quiet revolution in 2026. While the outside world debates which model has more parameters or higher benchmark scores, a scale application competition centered on "who can truly deploy AI" has quietly produced a clear winner. ## The Data Speaks: Eight Consecutive Weeks at the Top The latest monitoring data from OpenRouter, the world's largest AI model API aggregation platform, shows that for the week of June 15-21, global AI LLM total token usage reached **46.7 trillion tokens**, maintaining upward momentum for nine consecutive weeks. But what truly captures industry attention is the speed of China's AI LLM rise. That week, China's AI LLM weekly token usage reached **18.81 trillion tokens**, ranking first globally for eight consecutive weeks. Even more noteworthy is the quarter-on-quarter growth rate—**2.12%** weekly growth, maintaining positive quarter-on-quarter growth for four consecutive weeks. What does this mean? For comparison, US AI LLM weekly token usage was **5.76 trillion tokens** with only **0.70%** weekly growth. The Chinese market not only has 3.3 times the absolute volume of the US but also grows nearly three times faster. Eight consecutive weeks of leadership has firmly established China as the core player in global AI application deployment. ## From "Model Wars" to "Value Delivery" If one sentence could summarize the trajectory of China's AI LLM industry development, it would be: from the "model wars" of technical arms race to large-scale application deployment as the core focus. Looking back at 2023, when ChatGPT ignited the global LLM boom, hundreds of LLM projects emerged almost instantly in China. The competitive logic then was simple: whoever had more parameters and higher benchmark scores was the champion. Companies rushed to release new models and set benchmark records, with media headlines filled with "surpassing GPT-4." But by 2026, the rules of this game had quietly changed. **"Token usage" became the new measurement standard.** This is no longer a laboratory technical indicator but a real "voting with feet"—which model users are willing to pay for, which model is genuinely integrated into business processes, and which model generates value in real scenarios, all reflected in this number. Taking DeepSeek-V4-Flash as an example, this model maintained its top domestic ranking for five consecutive weeks with stable performance and excellent cost-effectiveness. Its single-week token usage for June 15-21 reached **4.94 trillion tokens**, a **12%** quarter-on-quarter increase, solidly ranking in the top tier. Xiaomi's MiMo-V2.5 achieved a significant leap, rising from fourth place last period to second place, with weekly token usage of **3.94 trillion tokens** and a **10%** quarter-on-quarter increase, with terminal scenario adaptation capabilities continuously gaining market recognition. While Tencent's Hy3preview slipped to fourth place, its weekly token usage was still **3.63 trillion tokens**. Competition for top industry positions is intensifying, with any slight slack potentially leading to being overtaken. ## Behind the Trillion Tokens: Who Is Using AI? Behind the data lies real industrial demand. According to IDC data, China's public cloud MaaS service market has entered an explosive period, with Volcano Engine holding **49.5%** market share and ranking first. Demand across the entire industry is growing rapidly, with Doubao LLM daily token usage increasing over **10 times** in the past year, and the number of enterprises with annual token usage exceeding **1 trillion** reaching **200**, doubling in half a year, covering the internet, manufacturing, finance, automotive, and other industries. What are these enterprises doing with AI? **The first scenario is code development.** Taking Volcano Engine's chip design RTL test as an example, Doubao 2.1 Pro ran continuously for nearly 18 hours, underwent 9 iterations, and completed the entire engineering process of simulation, testing, and verification. This would previously have required dozens of engineers working for several weeks. **The second scenario is intelligent Agent collaboration.** In the 3D virtual city scene demonstrated by Volcano Engine, over 500 intelligent Agents collaborated simultaneously, completing thousands of tool calls and generating over 100 buildings. This means AI has evolved from a "single-point tool" to a "collaboration network." **The third scenario is content creation.** Multimodal generation capabilities can support brands in rapidly producing marketing graphics and video content at low cost, with the Seedance video generation model already capable of directly outputting HD videos meeting marketing needs. ## Why Is China Leading? Analyst Zhang Yi pointed out that China's AI LLMs can lead globally for the long term thanks to two core advantages: **First, rich real economy scenarios.** China has the world's most abundant real economy scenarios and massive national-level traffic portals—weChat, short videos, smart government affairs, industrial intelligent manufacturing, smart homes, and other full-scenario applications provide LLMs with massive real interactive data and continuous iterative deployment soil. **Second, extreme cost control capability.** Domestic AI LLMs leverage the open-source ecosystem and extremely low API call pricing, lowering the access barriers for enterprises, developers, and small-to-medium merchants. Taking Doubao 2.1 Pro as an example, its comprehensive usage cost is nearly **80%** lower than international competitors, while Doubao 2.1 Turbo for high-frequency scenarios has its price reduced by another half. This transforms AI technology from a "high-end laboratory" attribute into **universal digital infrastructure** as fundamental as utilities. ## Farewell to Wild Growth: Industry Structure Reshaping Notably, the AI LLM industry has shifted from the early explosive expansion to mature development characterized by inventory optimization and quality enhancement. Data from Qichacha shows that as of June 22, there are **13,000** existing AI LLM-related enterprises in China, with changes in industry registration trends directly reflecting rational return in industrial development. In 2025, annual new registrations of AI LLM-related enterprises reached **395**, while new registrations in 2026 were only **24**. From the perspective of enterprise capital structure, the AI LLM industry's capital-intensive attributes are prominent, with leading enterprises having strong financial strength. Data shows that among domestic AI LLM-related enterprises, those with registered capital of **50 million yuan or above** account for **44.52%**, those with 10 million to 50 million yuan account for **30.72%**, and the two major categories together exceed **75%**. Those with registered capital **below 1 million yuan** account for only **3.21%**. The market elimination mechanism is gradually forming, with small and medium players lacking core technology and weak implementation capabilities accelerating their exit. Industry resources continue to concentrate toward leading high-quality enterprises, with industrial concentration continuously increasing. ## Outlook: From Scale Leadership to Comprehensive Leadership China's sustained global leadership in AI LLM token usage and the dual characteristics of high industrial concentration signal that China's AI industry has formed a mature positive development cycle. Looking ahead, with continuous breakthroughs in core technology, continuous improvement of industrial ecosystem, and continuous expansion of application scenarios, domestic AI LLM industry will continue to consolidate global competitive advantages, advancing from application scale leadership to **comprehensive leadership** in technology, ecosystem, and commercialization, continuously empowering digital economy transformation and upgrading, and steadily advancing China's AI industry toward a new stage of high-quality development. The next battleground might be Agents—when AI can independently plan tasks, independently call tools, and independently complete complex work, the ceiling for token usage may be just beginning to rise.
