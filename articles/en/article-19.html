AI Industry's Dual-Track Surge: Policy Dividends & IPO Frenzy in the Trillion-Dollar New Battlefield

📅 June 20, 2026 | 📖 9 min read

June 2026 marks an unprecedented "dual-track parallel" in the AI industry: On one side, China's eight ministries jointly issued documents promoting AI+consumption development; on the other, Anthropic and OpenAI's IPO race has entered a white-hot phase.

1. Policy Dividends: 17 Measures Targeting Consumer Scenarios

China's Ministry of Commerce and seven other departments released the "Implementation Opinions on Accelerating AI+Consumption Development," proposing 17 specific measures to drive AI into thousands of households.

Core Points:

• Expand consumption of AI phones, computers, smart home devices, humanoid robots

• Deep integration of AI with home living, eldercare, cultural tourism

• Promote AI applications in wholesale retail, e-commerce

Anthropic will hold its pre-IPO seminar on July 9, expecting Q2 revenue exceeding $10.9 billion with first-ever profit. OpenAI chose advertising monetization, launching ChatGPT ads in Japan.

3. Deep Logic Behind Dual-Track Parallel

Policy dividends and capital frenzy both point to the same trend: AI industry is transitioning from "technology-driven" to "application-driven." China's policy focuses on "consumer scenarios," while U.S. capital votes for "commercial closure."

Conclusion

The trillion-dollar new battlefield has no determined victor yet. But whoever can truly solve the "last mile" application deployment problem will seize the initiative in this historic competition.
